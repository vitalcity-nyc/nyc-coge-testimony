Who is testifying to the Commission on Government Efficiency

A working catalog of testimony and published positions aimed at COGE — Mayor Zohran Mamdani's 2026 Commission on Government Efficiency, the charter revision commission chaired by Patrick Gaspard that will put proposed New York City Charter amendments on the November 2026 ballot.

Snapshot as of June 23, 2026 Living document — hearings and written comments continue into mid-July 2026 Every entry links to a primary or news source

Is there one place that holds all the testimony?

No — not yet. There is no single public repository of COGE testimony.

It is easy to submit testimony but there is no posted corpus of what others have submitted. The commission streams its hearings and lists its schedule, but as of this snapshot it has not published a consolidated set of written submissions or transcripts. (The separate 2025 charter commission eventually posted a combined written-testimony PDF, so COGE may do the same later.) citymeetings.nyc, which transcribed the 2025 hearings, has not yet indexed the 2026 COGE hearings. So this catalog stitches together testimony from testifiers' own sites and news coverage.

Official channels: schedule and livestreams at nyc.gov/site/charter · submit written testimony via the COGE comment form or email CharterTestimony@citycharter.nyc.gov

How this was built, and what the labels mean

This catalog was assembled from public web research on June 23, 2026. Because COGE keeps no central testimony archive, entries were gathered from testifiers' own websites (the Comptroller, BetaNYC, council members and others) and from news coverage of the hearings. Nothing here is invented: every entry carries a working link to where the testimony or statement was found, and quotes are transcribed from those sources.

What the status labels mean

Formal testimony Delivered or submitted to COGE as testimony, confirmed by a primary source or specific news report.
Likely / intended Stated intent to testify, or strong indication of a submission, but the filed text was not located.
Reported statement Quoted in coverage previewing a hearing (for example a press call) rather than confirmed as delivered on the record.
Published position A public statement, blog post or explainer about COGE that was not confirmed as filed testimony — included because you asked for everything findable, clearly flagged.

What was deliberately excluded

This is the single biggest trap with COGE. There have been four New York City charter commissions in roughly two years, and the official site reuses the same web address for each. A great deal of widely cited charter testimony — from the Manhattan Institute, the Real Estate Board of New York, the Citizens Budget Commission, Public Advocate Jumaane Williams and others — was actually given to the 2024 and 2025 commissions, not to COGE. Those items were left out unless a 2026 COGE submission could be confirmed. If you see a familiar group missing, that is usually why.

A note on absent unions and business groups

Some major interests are shaping COGE from the inside rather than through testimony. DC 37's executive director Henry Garrido and the Partnership for New York City's Kathryn Wylde sit on the commission itself, so labor and big-business input is partly routed through commissioner seats and private feedback sessions, not public testimony.

Confidence and verification

Each card states a confidence level. Where a hearing date or the fact of delivery is uncertain, the card says so. For anything you intend to publish or rely on, confirm the verbatim wording and the submission against the original source or the official record.
